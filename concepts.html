Free will and the Body

I want to get better at video game development, but I don't put in the effort. Instead, I watch YouTube videos. I don't feel happy when doing that. My body doesn't have free will. Viewing it as a mechanical contraption doing simple tasks is more accurate than gathering the will power to work.

Learning from Tutorials

The Humane Rigging tutorial is a good tutorial. It explains the thought process behind each step and aims to develop a way of thinking. A good tutorial is the only way to growth. To augment a good tutorial, a reliable method of learning is necessary. With humane rigging, I write notes quickly at as close to the speed of the video as possible. Then I view the video again and solidify the notes. The first viewing is to soak in the way of thinking and the second is to clear up holes in my knowledge I wasn't aware of.

Level of Life

The level of my life when I'm not working has an effect on the length of my work. If I have decided to make Maggi or binge videos after I finish a certain task, I will rush the task. By rushing the task, I am at a net zero. I'm not good at quickly absorbing information. The presence of an attractive level of life at the end of my task incentivizes me to absorb information. This results in me completing the task on the surface, but not in me growing from my efforts. I have to remind myself of how much I want to grow to counter my desire for a short-term improvement in my level of life.
