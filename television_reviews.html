Attack on Titan

3x18: The Day
2019-06-20

Visuals

Gorgeous still life drawings during Grisha’s flashback.

Writing

Titan’s that appeared in the first episode of the series were shown to have originated in this episode. The Smiling Titan is Grisha’s first wife. Her eating Grisha’s second wife (Eren’s mother) is now a poetic and haunting scene, adding to the horror the viewer felt the first time watching the scene.

The Marley reveal makes Reiner, Bertholdt and Annie’s cloudy motivations now seem more reasonable. All the revelations fit neatly into the events that had happened in the show and don't contradict any plot devices or character motivations.

Ymir’s OVA which seemed confusing also fits neatly into the episode. Seemingly independent episodes are now connected and are important to the overall structure of the show.

Grisha’s origin also makes sense. He seems to appear outside the walls and the viewer is tricked into thinking that he was a wall resident who accidentally escaped.

Overall

Small scenes throughout the series now have very different tones in terms of how the viewer will watch them going back. Every character’s arc was meticulously written out by Isayama and each of them has an effect on the overall story. By telling the character perspective’s non-linearly and using Eren’s fading memories to do so is a stroke of genius. We avoid the flashback trope in favour of something that makes more sense.

3x19: Attack Titan
2019-06-24

Visuals

The galaxy image used in Ymir’s flashback gets thematic significance. The coordinate represents all the memory paths of all the Eldian’s. It is the intersection of these paths. Ymir’s flashback has a galaxy image, however even that has an intersection.

Writing

Dina Fritz is the Smiling Titan. This reveal serves to explain why Eren was able to control the titans in 2x12. According to the current rules of the world, only a descendant of royal blood can wield the Founding Titan’s powers. Eren was able to do it the moment he punched the Smiling Titan, despite not possessing royal blood. This is written off as him activating his power’s due to his heightened emotional state and a desire to survive. However, Isayama’s deft writing gives a proper explanation and closes any plausible loopholes in this portion of the story.

A user on reddit pointed out the significance of the reveal that Titan wielder’s can live for only 13 years.

Finally, they adapted the chapter where the Curse of Ymir was first mentioned, or the thirteen-year remaining lifespan curse.

For those who have not seen some discussions on how the number 13 is significant, lets do some math:

From the narrations in season 1 and the history books shown, around 100 years ago (specifically, 107 years from chapter 2 of the manga), the first ruler of the Walls, King Fritz the 145th created the walls to protect humanity.

When a someone gains a titan power, their remaining lifespan is set to 13 years due to “Ymir’s Curse”. Same number of years between the royal family’s succesion ritual.

“145th king” means there have been 145 wielders of the Founding Titan at that time. 145 x 13 = 1885. So 107 years ago, it has been 1885 years from Ymir Fritz.

At present time (1885 + 107) it would be 1992 years since Ymir Fritz.

Eren got his titan power at age 10, he’s supposed to die when he’s 23. Currently, Eren is 15 and has around 8 years left to live.

1992 + 8 = 2000

What was the title of the first episode again? Oh yeah, “To you, in 2000 years.” No biggie, nothing to see here.
